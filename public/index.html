Curriculum

What You Will Learn

SIX focused modules designed to take you from beginner to confident investor — from understanding how the Ghana Stock Exchange works, to identifying stocks worth buying, to building a profitable long-term portfolio.

Module 1: Introduction to the Ghana Stock Exchange

Understand what stocks are and why companies issue them. Learn how the GSE works — buyers, sellers, price discovery. Open your first brokerage account and prepare for your first trade.

Module 2: Reading Stocks & Financial Health

Read stock quotes, key metrics, and market indicators. Understand a company's financial health without jargon. Identify profitability, stability, and growth potential.

Module 3: Dividend & Value Growth Investing

Spot undervalued stocks and buy low for capital gains. Understand dividends, calculate yield, and build a steady-income portfolio. Combine both strategies for growth and cash flow.

Module 4: Simple Stock Valuation

Use valuation methods like P/E ratios, book value, and dividend models. Identify undervalued opportunities on the GSE. Avoid common beginner mistakes when picking stocks.

Module 5: Risk Management & Portfolio Strategy

Understand risk, volatility, and how to measure them. Build a diversified portfolio for long-term stability. Learn behavioral strategies to avoid panic and create your personal investment plan.

Module 6: Action & Your First Trade

Step-by-step guidance to buy your first stock. Apply dividend and value growth strategies in a real portfolio. Track performance and join a supportive community for ongoing guidance.

Module 7: Investing Vehicles

Learn how ETFs, index funds, and mutual funds work on the GSE. Understand the benefits of each and how to combine them. Choose the right brokerage and tools to diversify across multiple instruments.

Module 8: Intermediate Investing Concepts

Active vs passive investing — find the approach that fits you. Sector analysis, diversification strategies, introduction to bonds and fixed-income, and how macro factors like inflation impact your investments.
